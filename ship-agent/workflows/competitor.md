```bash

```

- If `$ARGUMENTS` names a specific competitor → Research that competitor
- If `$ARGUMENTS` names a category → Research that category
- If `$ARGUMENTS` is empty → Research the general product category from PROJECT.md

## 3. Research

Use web search to gather intelligence:

### 3a. If Specific Competitor
Search for:
- "[competitor name] features"
- "[competitor name] pricing"
- "[competitor name] reviews"
- "[competitor name] vs alternatives"

Analyze:
- What features do they offer?
- What's their pricing model?
- What do users love about them?
- What do users complain about?
- What's their target audience?

### 3b. If Category Research
Search for:
- "[category] tools [current year]"
- "best [category] apps"
- "[category] comparison"

Identify 3-5 competitors and analyze each briefly.

### 3c. Standard Checklist
For each competitor analyzed, evaluate:

| Factor | Question |
|--------|----------|
| **Market Position** | Leader, challenger, or niche? |
| **Target User** | Same as ours, adjacent, or different? |
| **Key Differentiator** | What's their unique angle? |
| **Feature Gaps** | What are they missing that we could exploit? |
| **Pricing** | Free, freemium, paid? How does it compare? |
| **Momentum** | Growing, stable, or declining? |

## 4. Identify Opportunities

Based on the research, identify:
- **Gaps we can exploit:** Features competitors don't have
- **Table stakes we're missing:** Features every competitor has that we don't
- **Differentiation angles:** Ways to position ourselves uniquely
- **Threats:** Competitors moving into our space

## 5. Ask the Builder

Present findings and ask:
1. "Any competitors I missed that you know about?"
2. "Which of these gaps feels most urgent to address?"
3. "Does this change how you think about the product's positioning?"
